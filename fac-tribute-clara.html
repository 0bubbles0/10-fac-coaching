Clara Schumann

Who was she?

Pianist

  • Clara lived from 1819 – 1897.
  • She was among the most prestigious concert pianists of the 19th century.
  • The child prodigy was taught by her father Friedrich Wieck, gave her first public concert aged 9 at the Leipzig Gewandhaus and launched her first European concert tour 3 years later.
  • She performed for over 60 years, shaping the Romantic era and recital programmes to the present day.
  • Her own compositions include a piano concerto, songs and other pieces for piano, choir and chamber ensembles.
  • She was the first piano teacher at the Frankfurt Conservatoire, raising technique standards.
  • She maintained close friendships with and was admired and by many contemporary musicians and artists such as:
    • Felix Mendelssohn-Bartholdy and his sister Fanny
    • Frédéric Chopin
    • Franz Liszt
    • Niccolò Paganini
    • Johann W.v. Goethe.
  • She very actively promoted their and her late husband’s repertoires.
  • If only we had audio recordings of her!

Powerhouse

  • Clara had 8 children.
  • She was the main bread-winner and care-taker of the family for most of her life.
  • She toured through 19th-century Europe, being her own manager, travel agent and talent.
  • As a teacher, she helped shape a new generation of largely female pianists.
  • She lost her hearing in later years.

Love

Robert Schumann

  • Enchanted by Clara’s playing, Robert left his law studies and became a live-in pupil of her father instead.
  • As part of his marriage proposal, Robert gifted her his (now famous) piano composition “Childhood Scenes”. After her father’s refusal, they had to seek a court intervention to be allowed to marry.
  • Robert and Clara had 8 children and later moved to Düsseldorf. While Robert was mostly known as a music critic and conductor, it was in fact Clara who was more famous during their lifetimes.
  • Clara supported Robert during his mental health struggles, while also raising their children and earning the family income through playing and teaching piano. Customary to the times, she was not allowed to visit him in hospital during the final years of his life.

Johannes Brahms

  • Aged 20, Johannes Brahms joined the Schumann household as a student to Robert. He became the couple’s closest confidante, all admiring each other’s musical talents.
  • During Robert’s illness, Brahms was among the few allowed to visit him in hospital. Throughout and after Robert’s death, Brahms supported Clara emotionally, in running the household and teaching the children.
  • After a refused proposal, they maintained a platonic yet passionate friendship, supporting each other artistically and corresponding for the rest of their lives, dying within a year of each other.
